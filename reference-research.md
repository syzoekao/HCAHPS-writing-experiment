# Reference research — HCAHPS overall star ratings 2017 vs 2024

**Last updated:** 2026-05-01
**Target journal:** Health Affairs (policy-forward); JAMA Health Forum and Health Affairs Scholar as alternates

---

## Gap statement after literature review

Hospital-level analyses of HCAHPS exist in two main forms: (a) cross-sectional determinant studies linking ratings to hospital and community characteristics, and (b) longitudinal description of national trends, especially the COVID-era decline. **No identified study models hospital-level *change* in overall star ratings between 2017 and 2024 with joint hospital, operational, and community-level covariates.** The closest prior work — Herrin et al. (2018) on community factors and Elliott/Beckett et al. (2023, 2025) on COVID-era trends — does not decompose levels-vs-change at the hospital level.

### Foundational reviews and the COVID-era trend literature (closest comparators)

| First author et al., year | Paper title | Study summary | DOI |

| ★ Elliott MN et al., 2023 | Changes in Patient Experiences of Hospital Care During the COVID-19 Pandemic | Cohort of 3.9M HCAHPS respondents from 3,381 hospitals comparing 2020–21 vs 2018–19. ### Star Ratings methodology and hospital-type disparities

| First author et al., year | Paper title | Study summary | DOI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gettel CJ et al., 2024 | Calculation of Overall Hospital Quality Star Ratings With and Without Inclusion of the Peer Grouping Step | Cross-sectional study of 3,076 hospitals. ## Journal recommendation: Health Affairs (primary)

**Why Health Affairs**
- Strongest policy reach in health-services research; explicitly oriented toward audiences that influence CMS payment design.
- Readers will care about your headline implication (community context explains levels but not change → cautions on payment use of star ratings).
- Format favors clear visual evidence — your maps + change-decomposition table fit naturally.
- Word limits are tight (~3,500–4,000 words, 6 exhibits); your story is compact enough to fit.

**Backups, in order of fit**
1. **JAMA Health Forum** — Elliott 2023 published here. Closest fit if reviewers see the work as extending the COVID-trend literature. Higher impact factor; faster turnaround.
2. **Health Affairs Scholar** — Beckett 2025 (CAH) published here. Open-access sister to Health Affairs; shorter cycle; same audience.
3. **Medical Care** — Beckett/Lehrman trends and Fitzpatrick Rosenbaum nursing both here. Best fit if methods become more central than policy.
4. **Health Services Research** — broader empirical scope, somewhat less policy reach.
5. **Milbank Quarterly** — for a more discursive policy framing if the empirical analysis is paired with a stronger argumentative narrative.
